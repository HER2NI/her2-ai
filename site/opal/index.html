What OPAL™ Delivers

OPAL™ creates a durable evidence layer for AI interactions: structured records that bind declared inputs, outputs, evaluator context, and provenance metadata into a recomputable artifact. The goal is simple: preserve what happened, under which conditions, so independent parties can verify and reconstruct behavior later.

Recomputable Integrity Records Non-Normative (Evidence-Only) Cross-Evaluator Temporal Causality
What OPAL™ produces
  • Integrity records: structured JSON evidence objects for interaction trajectories.
  • Cross-evaluator evidence: parallel records across evaluators without adjudication.
  • Temporal causality: ordered lineage across time (no overwrites; append-only).
  • Evidence bundles: signed bundles with manifest + recompute instructions.
What OPAL™ does not do
  • No output correction: OPAL™ does not regenerate or “fix” model responses.
  • No scoring authority: no certification, ranking, or compliance determination by default.
  • No model lock-in: works offline/CI/runtime; vendor-agnostic evidence layer.

How OPAL™ works
JSON-first Signed bundle Recompute instructions Cross-evaluator optional Temporal lineage

OPAL™ produces a chain of evidence by binding declared inputs, outputs, evaluator identity/config, and ordering metadata into a verifiable artifact. Records are designed to be portable and inspectable without relying on vendor internals.

Operational constraints
  • Works offline, in CI, or inside an agent runtime.
  • No external APIs required for the core evidence layer.
  • Does not require access to model weights or training data.

Note: LLM nondeterminism is expected. OPAL™ records what happened, and enables replay/recompute under declared conditions—without asserting correctness.

Important notes
  • Evidence-only by default: no certification, ranking, or compliance determination unless separately contracted.
  • No model interference: OPAL™ does not modify weights, suppress outputs, or enforce decisions.
  • Not clinical: not a diagnostic system and not medical advice.
  • Vendor-agnostic: designed to be independent of provider custody and proprietary logs.
